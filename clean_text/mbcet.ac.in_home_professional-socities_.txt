Indian Geotechnical Society (IGS)
The Indian Geotechnical Society (IGS) is a professional body that focuses on the field of geotechnical engineering in India. Founded in 1948, it has played a significant role in advancing geotechnical engineering knowledge and practices in the country. The society aims to promote the study, research, and practice of geotechnical engineering, as well as to provide a platform for professionals, researchers, and students to exchange ideas and information.
The IGS Student Chapter of MBCET, was inaugurated on 29/11/2023. It aims to organize seminars, conferences, workshops, quizzes and lectures by experts in the field to disseminate knowledge and keep the young geotechnical members updated with the latest developments.

Indian Plumbing Association(IPA)
Indian Plumbing Association: Driving Excellence in Plumbing Industry
The Indian Plumbing Association (IPA), established in 1993, is the apex body of plumbing professionals in India. With over 8,000 members comprising engineers, architects, consultants, contractors, and manufacturers, IPA aims to elevate the standards of plumbing design and practice in the country.
IPA focuses on promoting safe, sustainable, and water-efficient plumbing practices by providing technical guidance, conducting training programs, and organizing events like conferences and exhibitions. The association plays a pivotal role in creating awareness about public health and water conservation while advocating the use of globally accepted codes and standards.
One of IPA’s flagship initiatives is the Indian Plumbing Code (IPC), a comprehensive document tailored to Indian requirements. Additionally, IPA regularly collaborates with international organizations to exchange knowledge and foster advancements in plumbing technology.
With a vision to empower professionals and contribute to sustainable development, the IPA continues to lead efforts toward innovation and excellence in India’s plumbing industry.
